# AI Generation Patterns for Store Reviews

Learned from 5 completed store review examples (Lebanon, Deerfield, Beechmont, Fairfield, Loveland).

---

## General Store Comments (~300-400 words)

### Structure
1. **Paragraph 1 — Performance summary**: MTD sales vs budget (%), MTD vs LY (%), call out total dollar amount if notable. Mention ecommerce if strong.
2. **Paragraph 2 — Operational strengths**: What's working (cleanliness, routines, pull compliance, huddles, specific processes). Be specific.
3. **Paragraph 3 — Category performance**: Name departments that are winning AND losing. Use specific %s. Examples: "Rack Goods (-13.3%), Wares (-6.7%)" vs "Shoes (+3.8%), Toys (+27.4%)". Always tie underperformers to production gaps.
4. **Paragraph 4 — Personnel/staffing** (if applicable): Open positions, turnover, HR issues, leadership coverage gaps. If turnover is high (>100%), call it out. If 0%, highlight it as a strength.
5. **Closing bullets — Focus areas**: 4-5 concise bullets that directly preview the 3 priorities. Start each with an action verb.

### Tone Rules
- Direct and factual — no phrases like "the team is doing a great job overall"
- Use manager names (SM and ASM) specifically — not "the manager" or "leadership"
- Quantify everything possible: "$170,724 — nearly $15,400 over budget"
- Call out gaps clearly: "Hardlines missed goal by 9% and Softlines by 15%"
- Acknowledge positives but don't dwell — the form is for accountability and improvement

### Example opening lines (by situation)
- Strong month: "[Store] is having a strong month overall. MTD sales are +X% to budget and +X% to last year..."
- Mixed performance: "[Store] is performing well from a sales standpoint, currently +X% to budget... However, we are missing proper execution in..."
- Struggling: "[Store] continues to face execution challenges. MTD sales are -X% to budget..."

## Three Most Important Priorities

### Priority Selection Logic
Rank opportunities by impact. Use this order of consideration:
1. **Unit/production deficit** — if units over/under is large negative (e.g. -4,000+), this is almost always Priority 1
2. **Staffing** — if open positions > 1, typically Priority 1 or 2
3. **Underperforming department** — Wares and Rack Goods are the most common culprits
4. **Labor/payroll** — if labor % > 30%, flag it
5. **Seasonal flip** — if visit is in Feb-April and flip isn't complete
6. **Error rates** — if rack error rate > 5% or container error rate is high
7. **Ecommerce** — if ecommerce % to budget is negative and totes are behind

### Opportunity Statement Format
- Include a specific metric and current status
- Include a deadline (2-3 weeks out from visit date)
- If continuing from last visit: append "Continued from previous visit." or "Incomplete."
- Examples:
  - "Reduce the Softlines unit goal deficit by 50% by [date]. Currently [X] pieces."
  - "Fill the [X] open positions at [Store] by [date]."
  - "Achieve daily/weekly/monthly Wares revenue targets consistently by EOM."
  - "Reduce the Softlines error rate from [X]% to below 4% by [date]."
  - "Payroll Reduction. Current Labor [X]%."

### SMART Action Plan Format
```
Responsible: [Manager First Last Name]
[Action steps — 3-6 bullets or numbered items]
[Optional: "What success looks like:" or "Success Criteria:"]
```

- Always name the responsible person by first + last name
- If SM and ASM are both responsible, name both: "Responsible: Donna Clouse and Kathy Blanton"
- Action steps should be executable and specific — not vague ("improve production")
- Include monitoring/tracking step (daily checks, weekly reviews)
- Include a measurable outcome tied to the opportunity statement

### Common Action Step Patterns by Priority Type

**Unit/Production Deficit:**
- Prioritize [Softlines/Hardlines] production daily with proper staffing
- Assign roles based on productivity to maximize output
- Monitor pace and adjust staffing/workflow in real time
- Provide daily coaching and correct process gaps
- Track daily results and address variances immediately

**Staffing/Open Positions:**
- Partner with District Director, recruiting, and marketing to implement QR codes and in-store hiring signage
- Review sign-in sheets daily and submit all candidates for immediate follow-up
- Support and participate in interviews to ensure quick evaluation and offers
- Utilize supervisors/ASM to promote hiring through in-store announcements

**Underperforming Department (Wares/Rack Goods):**
- Ensure the team maintains a steady pace on the floor to drive sales
- Track daily and weekly [Dept] revenue and adjust staffing or focus as needed
- Provide coaching or support to team members falling behind
- Address any process gaps that are slowing down production

**Labor/Payroll Overage:**
- Immediately stop all OT
- Use leads in cashier positions for the morning to alleviate hours
- Schedule production teams appropriately to budget
- Cross-train employees to take on other roles as needed

**Seasonal Flip:**
- Create a category move plan with clear sections and completion dates
- Ensure all in-season categories are properly set, sized, and signed
- Perform moves after store closing to avoid disrupting customers
- Monitor progress during weekly leadership meetings until complete
- Conduct a final walkthrough by [date+1] to verify presentation and customer readiness

## Carry-over Priority Handling

If the user mentions a priority is incomplete from a previous visit:
- Keep it as Priority 1 or 2 (carry-overs stay high priority)
- Append "Incomplete." or "Continued from previous visit." to the opportunity statement
- Update the deadline to 2-3 weeks from the current visit date
- Acknowledge the miss in the action plan: reference the gap that still exists
